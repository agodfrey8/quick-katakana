Because of Japan’s worldwide cultural spread many people have an interest in travelling to or even moving to Japan. Often though, the infamously difficult Japanese language is seen as an impassable barrier and stops most plans before they even start. Few people have the time and ambition to learn enough Japanese to feel comfortable spending a long amount of time in the country.

However, there’s an aspect of the Japanese language called katakana that can be learned in a day, requires little understanding of the Japanese language, and is frequently used in Japan. In fact, it is so prevalent that some English-speakers live for years in Japan with only basic Japanese and a good understanding of katakana.

Katakana is the Japanese "alphabet" used to phonetically write the words borrowed from English. For example, words such as coffee and hotel. Knowing katakana makes it possible to find hidden English words in signs and menus all over Japan.

It doesn't take a lot to learn katakana, but it does help to understand how the three "alphabets" of Japanese relate to eachother.
