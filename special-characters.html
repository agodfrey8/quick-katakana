Special Characters

Dots and Circles

A character can have one of two marks next to it that changes the consonant of the syllable to another similar consonant.

Base Dots Circle
K – カ ka G – ガ ga
S – サ sa Z – ザ za
T – タ ta D – ダ da
H – ハ ha B – バ ba P – パ pa

Some characters have special rules here as well

Katakana Romaji Sound
ヂ “ji” or “di” jeep
ジ “ji” jeep
ヅ “dzu” or “du” or ”zu” zoo

ジ is almost always used instead of ヂ

ズ is almost always used instead of ヅ

A special use of this sound change is sometimes used in katakana.

ウ – u ヴ – v

However, it’s more common to use ‘b’ sounds in place of ‘v’ sounds.

  • ビデオ – bideo – video
  • ビタミン – bitamin – vitamin

Small Characters

A syllable ending with an ‘i’ sound followed by a small ヤ, ユ, or ヨ creates a new sound.

Base Two Syllables One Syllable
キ – ki キヤ – kiya キャ – kya キュ – kyu キョ – kyo
ギ – gi ギヤ – giya ギャ – gya ギュ – gyu ギョ – gyo

The same applies for ニ, ヒ, ミ, リ, ビ, ピ

  • キャベツ – kyabeji – cabbage
  • リニューアル – rinyūaru – renewal

However, there are slightly different rules for シ, ジ, チ

シ – shi シャ – sha シュ – shu ショ – sho
ジ – ji ジャ – ja ジュ – ju ジョ – jo
チ – chi チャ – cha チュ – chu チョ – cho
  • カシューナッツ – kashūnattsu – cashew nuts
  • ジャンプ – janpu – jump

Sometimes characters such asテ, フ, チ, and others will have a small vowel character next to them. By combining the consonant sound of the big character with the vowel sound of the little one, a new sound can be created that isn’t native to Japanese.

  • ティーンエージャー – tīneijā – teenager
  • ファイター – faitā – fighter
  • チェリー – cherī – cherry

Lines

A line after a syllable means a double vowel sound. In romaji, a line is usually written over the elongated vowel instead of writing the vowel twice.

  • カレー – karee/karē – curry
  • プレーヤー – pureeyaa/purēyā – player

Unlike English, this doesn’t change the vowel sound. It just makes the sound longer.

Small "tsu"

A small ツ before a syllable means a double consonant sound. In romaji, the elongated consonant is written twice.

  • バッグ – baggu – (computer) bug
  • Compared to: バグ – bagu – bag
  • カット – katto – (hair)cut

To double a consonant, hold your tongue in place for a moment before releasing it.

This is difficult for English speakers since long consonant sounds don’t exist in English.

Spaces

Circles between words represent spaces if they are necessary. Japanese doesn’t normally use spaces.
