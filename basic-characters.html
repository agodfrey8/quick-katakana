The Basic Characters

Vowels

Learning the base vowel sounds is most important. While English has many kinds of vowel sounds and even more ways to write them, Japanese only uses five vowel sounds.

Katakana ア イ ウ エ オ
Romanji a i u e o
Sound rod read rude red road

Vowels + Consonants

Other characters are created by combining adding a consonant in front of a vowel.

a i u e o
k カ ka キ ki ク ku ケ ke コ ko
s サsa シshi スsu セse ソso
t タ ta チ chi ツ tsu テ te ト to
n ナ na ニ ni ヌ nu ネ ne ノ no
h ハ ha ヒ hi フ fu ヘ he ホ ho
m マ ma ミ mi ム mu メ me モ mo
y ヤ ya ユ yu ヨ yo
r/l ラ ra リ ri ル ru レ re ロ ro
w ワ wa ヲ wo

Remember the vowel sounds don’t change. Don’t apply English phonics rules to romaji

Exceptions

A few characters deviate slightly from the established pattern. While there are two ways to write these syllables in romaji, they only have one sound

Katakana Romaji Sound
チ “chi” or “ti” cheap
シ “shi” or “si” sheep
ツ “tsu” or “tu” wetsuit

A few other characters are used to approximate one of two English sounds because the Japanese sound is somewhere between.

Katakana Romaji Sound
ラ “ra” or “la” ra / la
リ “ri” or “li” ri / li
ル “ru” or “lu” ru / lu
レ “re” or “le” re / le
ロ “ro” or “lo” ro / lo
フ “fu” fu / hu

Sometimes this causes two words to have the same katakana spelling.

ライト *right light
フード food hood

*Good example but not actually a borrowed word.

'n' and 'm'

There’s one consonant-only syllable in Japanese. However, it can be one of two sounds depending on the word.

ン n/m
