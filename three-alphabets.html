The Three “Alphabets”

The Japanese language consists of three kinds of written characters: kanji, hiragana, and katakana.

Kanji

Writing in Japan came from China.

Many existing Japanese words were given a Chinese character called kanji in Japanese.

These are the complicated pictograms people think of when they think Japanese is a difficult language.

  • 魚 – fish
  • 水 – water
  • 木 – tree

Chinese spoken language was very different from Japanese though. The characters only represented the Japanese meaning.

Later, a Japanese writing system called man’yogana was created by using Chinese characters for their phonetic meaning only.

Hiragana

Man’yogana was tedious so two other systems were developed.

The first was hiragana which came from simplifying the cursive forms of kanji. A writing style that was specifically for women.

Nowadays it’s mostly used for particles (small words that affect grammatical meaning of a sentence), for writing Japanese words without using kanji (if the kanji isn’t common), and for furigana (especially for younger readers)

  • 魚 – さかな – fish
  • 水 – みず – water
  • 木 – もく – tree

One character represents one syllable, a consonant vowel pair

Hiragana can be written using roman characters (romaji) also.

  • 魚 – さかな – sakana – fish
  • 水 – みず – mizu – water
  • 木 – もく – moku – tree

Unlike English, a character in Japanese always has the same pronunciation.

Often Japanese words are pronounced incorrectly in English (karaoke, karate, sake)

Hiragana is much easier to read than one might expect because it requires less memorizing than kanji and less pronunciation rules than English.

However, hiragana still requires a knowledge of Japanese vocabulary

Katakana

The second system was katakana. It was a similar Man’yogana simplification that was developed by monks.

It was used by men for official documents.

It could be thought of as English cursive being used in more formal situations

Phonetically hiragana and katakana work exactly the same. Just different characters for the same syllables.

Nowadays it’s mostly used for words that have been borrowed from other cultures. Many of which are English.

Instead of creating a new word, Japanese phonetics are used to approximate the phonetics of the borrowed word.

The word is then usually written in katakana instead of hiragana to separate it as a borrowed word.

  • アメリカ – amerika - America
  • アイスクリーム – aisukurīmu – ice cream
  • ゲーム・オブ・スローンズ – gēmu obu surōnzu – Game of Thrones

This makes katakana even easier to read then hiragana because it doesn’t require any knowledge of Japanese vocabulary. It only requires memorizing the characters and pronouncing the borrowed word until it sounds like a familiar English word.
