Potential Problems

While katakana doesn’t require learning Japanese vocabulary, the English word being used isn’t always immediately clear.

Character Confusion

The following characters are very easy to confuse. Especially when they are printed.

ン n ソ so
シ shi ツ tsu

Keep these characters in mind while trying to read katakana. If something isn’t matching an English word you know, consider changing a character for a similar looking character and trying again.

Phonetic Limitations

Keep the limitations of the phonetics in mind. Sometimes borrowed words have a few extra sounds in them because of the syllables available.

  • ツナ – tsuna – tuna
  • シーディー – shīdī – CD

This is especially true when a borrowed word ends in a consonant other the ‘n’ or ‘m.’ Japanese adds an extra vowel sound to the end of these words.

  • カード – kādo – card
  • スポーツ – supōtsu – sports

Sometimes the limited vowel sounds of Japanese will cause a borrowed word to sound like another English word.

  • カット – katto – (hair)cut (NOT cat)

Sometimes an English word becomes very complicated in Japanese and becomes almost impossible to link back to the original.

  • マクドナルド – Makudonarudo – McDonald’s

Cultural Differences

Sometimes Japanese speakers don’t use all of an English word. It’s possible to realize these abbreviations with some thought and context though.

  • アパート – apaato – apart(ment)
  • エアコン – eacon – air con(ditioner)
  • パソコン – pasocon – perso(nal) com(puter)

Sometimes when part of a word is used it can sound like a different English word. Use the context of the situation to help.

  • サンド – sando – sand(wich)
  • アイス – aisu – ice (cream)

Sometimes Japanese uses English words in unique ways. The more time you spend in Japan the more of these you’ll learn through experience.

  • アメリカンドッグ – amerikandoggu – American dog (a corn dog)
  • ファイト – faito – fight (“Do your best!”)
  • バイキング – baikingu – viking (buffet)
  • Sometimes written: ヴァイキング – vaikingu
  • tension

Sometimes words are borrowed from another language

  • アルバイト – aurbaito – (part-time) work – German
  • パン – pan – bread – Spanish/French
