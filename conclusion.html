Closing Thoughts

Some English is available in Japan.

Learning English is fairly common in Japan, but pronunciation is often closer to katakana than native English.

Knowing the rules of katakana can make it easier to understand a helpful or friendly Japanese English-speaker.

Remember, a character is always pronounced the same. If you want to speak a borrowed word in Japanese, make sure to use the Japanese pronunciation to be understood easier.

Understanding katakana and romanji can also help decipher translation errors on English signs and menus.
