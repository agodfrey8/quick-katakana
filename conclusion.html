Conclusion

Japan is a wonderful country to visit and explore. It has a rich culture that is weaved through every city street and country road. It is a shame so many allow the language to intimidate them from experiencing it for themselves.

English is actually quite readily available in most parts of Japan. Many signs contain some English and many menus have an English version. Combine that with a good understanding of katakana, and Japan becomes surprisingly accessible. Knowing katakana can even help decipher some English translation errors.

Additionally, learning English is fairly common in Japan, but Japanese speakers of English often lack confidence. Japanese people often struggle with speaking and listening in English even when it involves words they know the meaning of. This is because, English pronunciation in Japan is often closer to katakana than native English. Adapting to the katakana pronunciation can sometimes be the key to being understood in Japan.

Katakana is a great way to make a trip to Japan easier, but it is no replacement for actually learning Japanese. It is still highly recommended that those interested in spending time in Japan learn at least a couple key Japanese phrases. If nothing else, it's a quick way to make some friends while you visit. Japanese people are infamously impressed by foreigners making an effort to speak Japanese.
